Codebase-backed explainer

How the X algorithm thinks, and how to post for it

A practical, non-technical guide based on this repository's For You feed architecture: Thunder for in-network posts, Phoenix for discovery, and a Grok-derived transformer that predicts what each viewer is likely to do.

The main idea

X does not simply rank posts by likes. The feed first gathers candidates, predicts many kinds of engagement for each viewer, then filters and diversifies the result. The best strategy is to create posts that earn real conversation, attention, sharing, follows, and low negative feedback from the right audience.

How ranking works

The For You feed in five steps

The repository's `home-mixer` pipeline combines candidate retrieval, hydration, filtering, scoring, selection, and final safety checks.

1

Understand the viewer

Query hydrators fetch recent engagement history, following data, and user features.

2

Gather candidates

Thunder supplies in-network posts. Phoenix Retrieval finds out-of-network posts through similarity search.

3

Filter obvious misses

The system removes duplicates, old posts, self-posts, muted content, blocked authors, already-seen posts, and inaccessible content.

4

Predict engagement

Phoenix predicts actions like reply, repost, click, share, dwell, follow, and negative feedback.

5

Rank and diversify

Scores are weighted, repeated authors are dampened, out-of-network posts are adjusted, and top candidates are selected.

What the model rewards

Signals to create for, and signals to avoid

The weighted scorer combines many predicted actions. Exact production weights are not present in this repository, so this is about direction, not secret numeric formulas.

Positive signals

Replies Reposts Quotes Likes Clicks Profile clicks Dwell Shares DM shares Copy-link shares Follows Qualified video views

Negative signals

Not interested Mute author Block author Report Spam-like repetition Policy or safety violations
Creator strategy

A posting strategy anyone can follow

Do not try to "hack" the algorithm. Give the model better reasons to predict that your post will satisfy a specific viewer.

1. Pick a clear lane

Choose one to three topics you want to be known for. Clear topical identity helps discovery systems match you with interested people.

2. Write for replies

Ask useful, specific questions. Invite examples, tradeoffs, and opinions that start real conversation.

3. Earn dwell time

Use short stories, lists, before-and-after breakdowns, and practical frameworks that make people pause and read.

4. Be shareable

Package ideas so someone would send them to a friend: checklists, templates, definitions, examples, and useful contrasts.

5. Build follower gravity

In-network posts matter structurally. Make your bio, pinned post, and recurring value promise obvious.

6. Avoid negative feedback

Misleading hooks, rage bait, repetition, and irrelevant tagging can earn mutes, blocks, reports, and "not interested" actions.

Important context

What this explainer can and cannot claim

This is not a promise of reach. It is a practical interpretation of the repository's architecture and visible scorer behavior. Exact production weights, thresholds, safety rules, and experimentation layers are not fully visible in this checkout.

Treat specific public claims about exact 2026 weights with caution unless X publishes them directly. The safer strategy is durable: relevance, conversation, attention, sharing, follows, and low negative feedback.
